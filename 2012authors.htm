Author Luncheons

Thursday, January 26, 2012
Tom Corcoran first moved to Florida in 1970. He has been a disc jockey, bartender, naval  officer, screenwriter, freelance photographer and journalist. In addition to his Alex Rutledge mysteries, he has written non-fiction auto books, published Jimmy Buffett – The Key West Years, and has published volumes of his Key West photographs. Alex Rutledge, his protagonist, is a forensic photographer who operates in a Key West that tourists seldom see.

 

 

Thursday, February 23, 2012
Phyllis Smallman's love of books led to various library jobs in southern Ontario towns like Brantford, Grimsby, and Ancaster, where she hid out in the stacks reading mysteries. She then turned her hand to writing them, winning the first Arthur Ellis award for best unpublished manuscript, which lead to a publishing contract. Smallman lives in Englewood, Florida six months of the year and writes mysteries about the Mangrove coast of Florida.

 

 

Thursday, March 22, 2012
Nancy J. Cohen has an undergraduate degree from University of Rochester and a Master’s degree from the University of California. She is the author of the Bad Hair Day mystery series. In this mystery series, the protagonist, Marla Shore, is a local beauty salon owner and part-time sleuth. Nancy has also written romance novels under the pseudonym Nancy Cane. Cohen lives in South Florida with her husband.
