2013 Beverly Lewis Author Event

Beverly Lewis spoke to a crowded room at the Bonita Springs Fire Dept. on Bonita Grande and her off the cuff speech was met with applause. Ms. Lewis made Bonita Springs one of her stops on a book tour to promote her new book “The Guardian”. Ms. Lewis writes exclusively about the Amish community of Lancaster County, Pennsylvania, a place near to where she was raised. She gave some insight into her writing style and shared that writing is who “she is”. She began writing at age 7 and has written for children and adults ever since. Her book “The Shunning” was made into a Hallmark movie and will soon be available on DVD. Ms. Lewis shared funny stories about people she has met through the years that are Amish and told how their world is changing by the encroachment of the outside world. Her lecture was funny and enlightening about a world most of us know little.

2013 Friends' Member Appreciation Tea

The members that attended the Appreciation Tea held on March 28 were treated to goodies and given a chance to interact informally with the members of the Friends Board of Directors. Several suggestions were made regarding the menus for the luncheons and for future authors. The Board members were excited with the enthusiasm expressed and all of the suggestions will be taken into consideration.

2013 Volunteer Luncheon

Each year in February, we honor our volunteers with a luncheon. The staff brings in a favorite side dish or dessert and the Friends of the Library supply a cake, sub sandwiches and beverages. One of the best parts of the luncheon (besides good food) is that volunteers get to meet other volunteers. They all work different days each week and while they all know the jobs, they don’t know each other. This luncheon gives them a chance to talk about their library tasks and get to know each other. Catherine Vaughn, Marilyn Graham and Sheldon Kaye were also in attendance.
